Compelling design goes beyond augmenting the utility of products or services, or meeting the needs of a user group. Compelling design is about creating real change in the people you touch through the experiences you develop. It is not only human-centred; it is purpose-led.

For me, that higher purpose is building community. In a world facing increased loneliness, polarization, and isolation, I believe it’s imperative to understand how we can bridge these gaps and help people feel more connected and fulfilled with one another. I also believe that championing community-focused and purpose-led design requires a multi-disciplinary lens to succeed.

With a career in strategy consulting and emerging technologies at Monitor Deloitte, I know how to build future-looking, commercially viable solutions. Thanks to my extensive experience standing up non-profit startups and my most recent venture into the world of UX design and research at Carnegie Mellon University's Master of Human-Computer Interaction program, I also understand how to bring a people-first purpose to those solutions.

My unique background allows me to combine my knowledge and passions in strategy, community-building, and UX to design solutions that drive business value, deliver societal change, and delight users. I’m excited to focus my future work on emerging technologies as they open up ways for communities to connect that are richer and more inclusive than ever before. More importantly, they provide exciting new avenues to focus on a personal goal of mine:

How can we bring people together?
